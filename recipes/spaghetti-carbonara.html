Spaghetti

A classic Roman dish, Spaghetti Carbonara is a creamy pasta delight made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It's a simple yet indulgent dish that's perfect for weeknight dinners or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Parsley (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and then drain the pasta.
  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, add olive oil and minced garlic. Once fragrant, add the diced pancetta. Cook until it's crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Add the drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to combine.
  5. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour in the egg mixture, stirring rapidly to ensure the eggs don't scramble. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you get a creamy sauce consistency.
  6. Season with salt and more pepper if needed. Garnish with parsley if desired.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy!
