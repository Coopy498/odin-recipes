Pizza

A timeless pizza recipe that highlights the simplicity and freshness of its ingredients. The Margherita pizza features a crispy crust, tangy tomato sauce, melted mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil leaves. It's a tribute to the colors of the Italian flag and a testament to the idea that sometimes, less is more.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (preferably homemade or a good quality store-bought one)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional: a pinch of red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to its highest setting, usually around 475¬∞F (245¬∞C) to 500¬∞F (260¬∞C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.
  2. Prepare Dough: On a floured surface, roll out the pizza dough to your desired thickness, typically about 1/4-inch thick for a crispy crust. If you're using store-bought dough, follow the instructions on the package.
  3. Add Sauce: Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  4. Cheese It Up: Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the tomato sauce.
  5. Bake: If using a pizza stone, carefully transfer the pizza onto the preheated stone using a pizza peel or an inverted baking sheet. If not using a stone, place the pizza on a baking sheet. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  6. Final Touches: Once out of the oven, scatter fresh basil leaves over the top. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with a pinch of salt, and add red pepper flakes if desired.
  7. Serve: Slice using a pizza cutter and serve immediately. Enjoy your classic Margherita pizza!
